Present Tenses

Present Simple — habits, facts, general truths

I study English every day.

Present Continuous — happening now or temporary

I am studying English now.

Present Perfect — past action with present relevance

I have studied English for 2 years.

Present Perfect Continuous — ongoing action from past to now

I have been studying English since 2024.

Past Tenses

Past Simple — completed action at a specific time

I studied English yesterday.

Past Continuous — action in progress at a past time

I was studying when he called.

Past Perfect — action completed before another past action

I had finished before he arrived.

Past Perfect Continuous — ongoing action before a past point

I had been studying for 3 hours before dinner.

Future Tenses

Future Simple — prediction or instant decision

I will study tomorrow.

Future Continuous — action in progress at a future time

I will be studying at 8 PM.

Future Perfect — action completed before a future point

I will have finished by next week.

Future Perfect Continuous — duration up to a future point

I will have been studying for 2 years by 2027.

Subject–Verb Agreement

✔ He plays football.
✗ He play football.
✔ The number of students has increased.
✔ A number of students have withdrawn.
✔ The committee has made its decision.

Articles — a / an / the / zero

a/an = first mention or non-specific  ·  the = specific or already known  ·  no article = general plural/uncountable

I saw a dog. The dog was black. (a = first mention → the = known)
I ate an apple. (an before vowel sound)
Education is essential for development. (no article — general)
The internet has changed communication. (unique thing — use the)

Countable & Uncountable Nouns

Countable: book, student, apple — can be plural. Use many/few.

Uncountable: water, information, advice, furniture — no plural. Use much/little.

✗ many informations    ✔ much information
✗ many advices    ✔ a lot of advice
✗ two furnitures    ✔ two pieces of furniture

Modal Verbs

Modal Use Example
can ability I can swim.
could past ability I could swim at age 5.
may / might possibility It might rain tomorrow.
must obligation You must submit the form.
should advice You should study harder.
would hypothetical I would travel if I could.

Question Forms — important for Speaking

Where do you live?
Why are you learning English?
How long have you studied English?
What would you do if you had more time?

Indirect questions — word order is NOT inverted.

✗ I want to know where does she live.
✔ I want to know where she lives.

Conjunctions

Coordinating (FANBOYS): for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so

Technology creates jobs, but it also displaces workers.

Subordinating: because, although, while, since, whereas, unless, even though

Although it was raining, we went outside.
Since the population is ageing, healthcare costs are rising.

Conditionals

Type Structure Example
Zero if + present, present If you heat water, it boils.
First if + present, will If it rains, I will stay home.
Second if + past, would If I were rich, I would travel.
Third if + past perfect, would have If I had studied, I would have passed.
Mixed if + past perfect, would If I had studied medicine, I would be a doctor now.

Passive Voice

Structure: Subject + be + past participle. Use when the doer is unknown or unimportant.

Active: The company built the bridge.
Passive: The bridge was built by the company.
The data was collected from 500 participants.
More funding should be allocated to public health.

Reported Speech

Tense shifts back one step: Present → Past  ·  Will → Would  ·  Can → Could

Direct: He said, "I am tired."
Reported: He said that he was tired.
Direct: She said, "I will come tomorrow."
Reported: She said that she would come the next day.

Relative Clauses

Defining (no commas) — who/that/which — restricts meaning.

The man who lives next door is a doctor.
The book that I bought is interesting.

Non-defining (with commas) — who/which — adds extra info.

Professor Lee, who chairs the department, has written 30 papers.

where = place  ·  when = time  ·  whose = possession

The city where I live is crowded.

Comparatives & Superlatives

Comparative: adjective + -er / more + adjective + than

Dhaka is bigger than Khulna.
Urban areas are more densely populated than rural ones.

Superlative: the + adjective + -est / the most + adjective

Dhaka is the biggest city in Bangladesh.

As … as (equal comparison)

The 2020 rate was not as high as in 2019.

Complex Sentences — Band 7+

Combine a main clause with one or more subordinate clauses.

Although I was tired, I completed my homework because the deadline was approaching.
Since technology has improved communication, businesses that operate globally have become more efficient.

Linking Words

Function Words
Addition furthermore, moreover, in addition, additionally
Contrast however, nevertheless, on the other hand, whereas
Cause / Effect therefore, thus, as a result, consequently, owing to
Example for instance, for example, such as, in particular
Conclusion in conclusion, to sum up, overall, in summary

Gerunds & Infinitives

Gerund (verb + -ing) — used as subject or after: enjoy, avoid, consider, suggest

I enjoy reading. / Reducing pollution is a global priority.

Infinitive (to + verb) — after: want, need, decide, plan, hope + adjectives

I want to study. / It is important to consider the impact.

Meaning changes with stop / remember / try

She stopped to think. (paused in order to think)
She stopped thinking. (no longer thought about it)

Advanced Grammar — Band 8+

Inversion — negative adverb at front for emphasis

Never have I seen such a beautiful place.
Not only does technology save time, but it also reduces costs.

Cleft sentences — emphasise one part

What I enjoy most is reading books.
It is education that makes the biggest difference.

Participle clauses — shorten complex sentences

Having completed the exam, I felt relieved.
Students studying abroad often face challenges. (reduced relative clause)

IELTS Writing Structures

Opinion / Argument

I believe that technology has improved our lives significantly.
In my opinion, the benefits of globalisation outweigh the drawbacks.

Advantage / Disadvantage

One major advantage is that it creates employment opportunities.
A significant drawback is the environmental impact.

Discussion (both sides)

On the one hand, remote work offers greater flexibility.
On the other hand, it can lead to social isolation.

IELTS Basic Phrases & Idioms

Giving Opinion

In my opinion, learning English is very important.
From my perspective, technology has improved education.

Agreeing

I completely agree with this idea.
That's absolutely true in many cases.

Disagreeing

I don't entirely agree with this point.
I see it differently because it depends on the situation.

Useful Speaking Fillers

Well, let me think for a moment.
That's an interesting question.
To be honest, I haven't thought about that before.

Common Idioms

A piece of cake — very easy (The exam was a piece of cake.)
Once in a blue moon — very rarely (I eat fast food once in a blue moon.)
Break the ice — start a conversation (He told a joke to break the ice.)
Hit the books — study hard (I need to hit the books for IELTS.)
Cost an arm and a leg — very expensive (Studying abroad costs an arm and a leg.)

All 12 Tenses at a Glance
Tense Structure Example
Present Simple V / V+s I study every day.
Present Continuous am/is/are + V-ing I am studying now.
Present Perfect have/has + V3 I have studied for 2 years.
Present Perfect Continuous have/has been + V-ing I have been studying since 2024.
Past Simple V2 I studied yesterday.
Past Continuous was/were + V-ing I was studying when he called.
Past Perfect had + V3 I had finished before he arrived.
Past Perfect Continuous had been + V-ing I had been studying for 3 hours.
Future Simple will + V I will study tomorrow.
Future Continuous will be + V-ing I will be studying at 8 PM.
Future Perfect will have + V3 I will have finished by next week.
Future Perfect Continuous will have been + V-ing I will have been studying for 2 years by 2027.

Key: V = base verb  ·  V2 = past form (studied)  ·  V3 = past participle (studied / gone / written)  ·  V-ing = present participle

Band 8+ Advanced Structures

Use these to impress examiners — but only when they sound natural, not forced.

1 Inversion

Negative adverb moved to front for emphasis — requires auxiliary before subject.

Never have I seen such a beautiful place.
Not only does technology save time, but it also reduces costs.
Rarely do governments prioritise long-term solutions.

2 Cleft Sentences

Emphasise one part of a sentence using "What … is" or "It is … that".

What I enjoy most is reading books.
It is education that makes the biggest difference to society.
What concerns me most is the lack of government action.

3 Participle Clauses

Shorten two clauses into one, making sentences more concise and academic.

Having completed the exam, I felt relieved. (= After I had completed…)
Faced with rising costs, many families struggle. (= Because they are faced with…)

4 Reduced Relative Clauses

Drop "who/that is" when the meaning is clear.

Students studying abroad often face challenges. (= Students who study abroad…)
The policy introduced in 2018 was highly controversial. (= The policy that was introduced…)
